METHODOLOGY / FOR THE PROFESSOR

Breeden–Litzenberger digital approximation

Why a tight call spread converges to a digital option as the strikes tighten.

DISCRETE FORM (WHAT WE USE)

P(ST > K) ≈ C(K) − C(K+ε) / ε

C(K) = call price at strike K; ε = spread width

LIMITING CASE (BREEDEN–LITZENBERGER 1978)

f(K) = ∂2C / ∂K2

As ε→0 the call spread recovers the
full risk-neutral density f(K) at strike K.

Our prototype uses the discrete approximation with adjacent listed strikes — defensible, inspectable in the UI, but coarser than a smoothed call surface.

WHY THIS WORKS

  • A long-K / short-(K+ε) call spread pays $1 per ε of width if ST > K, else $0 — a scaled binary.
  • By no-arbitrage, the spread cost equals the risk-neutral expected payoff under Q.
  • Polymarket YES is a binary, traded directly. Discrepancy ⇒ tradable edge.

KNOWN LIMITS

  • Coarse ε: when spread width / spot > 5%, basis risk is material. UI flags this.
  • Expiry mismatch: Poly & Deribit dates rarely align; nearest-listed used; warned if gap > 7d.
  • Smoothing: cubic-spline of the surface is the obvious next step. Listed in future work.
